The second-term Trump administration is not just generating noise. It is hitting specific pillars.

First, DEI. Federal DEI expansion stopped being a one-way ratchet and turned into a live legal and administrative fight. [C]
Evidence:
- https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/01/ending-radical-and-wasteful-government-dei-programs-and-preferencing/

Second, climate and energy. The administration is pushing fossil approvals, emergency framing, and rollback pressure against the net-zero governance model. [C]
Evidence:
- https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/04/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-declares-national-emergency-to-increase-our-competitive-edge-protect-our-sovereignty-and-strengthen-our-national-and-economic-security/

Third, trade. Tariffs are being used as leverage to punish offshoring, force concessions, and reprice access to the U.S. market.

Fourth, the compliance network. The TNI-related antitrust intervention matters because it signals that viewpoint collusion can be treated as a legal problem rather than a noble public-health or public-safety exception. [C]
Evidence:
- https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/justice-department-files-statement-interest-suppression-competition-marketplace-ideas

Fifth, the money layer. DOGE-style auditing, fraud exposure, program-integrity drives, and aid review threaten the bureaucratic and NGO surfaces that grew around high-spend governance.

Sixth, immigration. Harder asylum and border positions matter because they attack future lock-in, not only current numbers.

This is the right way to describe the conflict. It is not one man fighting an abstract vibe cloud. It is an administration pressuring specific institutional rails that had been treated as nearly irreversible.
