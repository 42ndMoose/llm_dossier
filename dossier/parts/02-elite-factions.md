There is not one all-controlling world brain. There are rival blocs inside one shared system.

Bloc one is the Davos, ESG, DEI, climate-finance side. It includes WEF-linked governance culture, major asset managers, regulatory language around net zero and inclusion, large NGOs, academic hubs, and much of legacy media.

Bloc two is the nationalist, America First, resource-capital side. It is more comfortable with tariffs, fossil fuels, industrial policy, sovereignty language, harder immigration controls, and open conflict with DEI and ESG mandates.

The conflict is real, but it is constrained. Both sides still operate inside the same courts, bureaucracies, capital markets, media platforms, and global supply chains.

There is also an older bipartisan economic script underneath both sides. For decades, the default model rewarded offshoring, cheap labor, asset-light management, and financial engineering. The second-term Trump administration is disruptive because it breaks that script more openly than the old Republican donor class ever wanted.

That matters for the dossier because it explains why resistance to Trump is not only moral or cultural. Some of it is structural. Tariffs, onshoring, and harder borders hit business models, governance frameworks, and political coalitions that were built around the older globalized arrangement.
