10. What the second-term Trump administration is actually hitting

The second-term Trump administration is not just generating noise. It is hitting specific pillars.

First, DEI. Federal DEI expansion stopped being a one-way ratchet and turned into a live legal and administrative fight.

Second, climate and energy. The administration is pushing fossil approvals, emergency framing, and rollback pressure against the net-zero governance model.

Third, trade. Tariffs are being used as leverage to punish offshoring, force concessions, and reprice access to the U.S. market.

Fourth, the compliance network. The TNI-related antitrust intervention matters because it signals that viewpoint collusion can be treated as a legal problem rather than a noble exception.

Fifth, the money layer. DOGE-style auditing, fraud exposure, program-integrity drives, and aid review threaten the bureaucratic and NGO surfaces that grew around high-spend governance.

Sixth, immigration. Harder asylum and border positions matter because they attack future lock-in, not only current numbers.

10.1 Demonization, delegitimization, and real-world harm

This is where the original dossier was strongest and where the rebuild needed to carry more forward.

DHS repeatedly said assaults on ICE officers surged in 2025, moving from 413% to 500%, then 830%, and later more than 1000% in official department messaging as the year went on.

The DOJ charged Rahmanullah Lakanwal in the ambush-style shooting near the White House that killed National Guardsman Sarah Beckstrom and seriously injured National Guardsman Andrew Wolfe.

The FBI and DOJ formally treated the Butler and West Palm Beach incidents as attempted assassinations of Donald Trump, not as random disturbances.

The FBI and DOJ also treated Charlie Kirk's killing as a real political assassination event, not a rumor or internet myth.

The dossier's analytical point is not that every media figure ordered these events. The point is that when a class of institutions spends years delegitimizing ICE, treating Trump as uniquely evil, and painting nationalist figures as existential threats, the resulting climate is not politically neutral.

That is why this section belongs in the dossier. It is not only about policy. It is about the real-world cost of one-sided moral permission structures.
