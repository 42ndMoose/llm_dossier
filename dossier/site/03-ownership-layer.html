3. Ownership layer: Big Three and legacy media

The Big Three in this dossier means **BlackRock, Vanguard, and State Street**. They repeatedly appear as major institutional holders across large public companies, including major media parents.

The concrete media-parent layer is straightforward:
- Comcast controls NBC, MSNBC, and CNBC.
- Disney controls ABC and ESPN.
- Warner Bros. Discovery controls CNN.
- Fox Corporation controls Fox News and Fox broadcast.
- Sinclair controls a large local-TV station network.

Each of those companies can be audited through SEC proxy statements and 13G filings, where the Big Three repeatedly show up as major institutional holders.

Fox is the best reminder that ownership influence is not the same thing as direct command. The Murdoch structure preserves control through dual-class shares even while large institutions still hold major economic stakes.

The right split is:
1. owners and large institutions,
2. corporate management and boards,
3. on-air talent and editorial operators.

That distinction matters because weak versions of this topic get laughed out of the room. The serious version is not "Larry Fink scripts every segment." The serious version is that **ownership concentration, board pressure, stewardship norms, legal-risk sensitivity, and shared elite social worlds all narrow the range long before a script reaches the teleprompter**.

So the ownership layer does not explain everything. It explains why so many outlets begin from the same class position, defend the same ideological furniture, and panic at the same kinds of disruption.
