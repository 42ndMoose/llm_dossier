3. Ownership and governance layers

The Big Three in this dossier means BlackRock, Vanguard, and State Street. They repeatedly appear as major institutional holders across large public companies, including major media parents.

That ownership layer matters, but it should be described precisely. It is leverage at board, governance, and risk level. It is not the same thing as a daily newsroom command feed.

The cleaner split is:
1. owners and large institutions,
2. corporate management and boards,
3. on-air talent and editorial operators.

This is why the dossier should not overclaim. The ownership layer helps explain convergence in incentives, especially around risk, governance, and approved norms. It does not mean a fund manager personally scripts every segment on CNN, Fox, or MSNBC.

The more serious point is that diversified ownership, governance pressure, and shared elite social worlds can narrow the acceptable range without a literal boss barking orders. That is enough to matter.
